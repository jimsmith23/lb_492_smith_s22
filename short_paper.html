Evolutionary Medicine Research Mini-Proposal

In this short-medium writing exercise (800 - 1000 words; 4 pages, double-spaced), each student will propose research to answer a question in Evolutionary Medicine. In addition to providing the first chance for you to write formally for the course, our goals in assigning this exercise are to orient students to topics in evolutionary medicine that are currently being researched, and to provide a mechanism for students to engage with the course textbook and, from there, the primary literature.

There are four possible research areas where funding looks promising, and each student should choose one of these topic areas for their research proposal. These topic areas are:

  • Ageing and Senescence
  • Evolution of Infectious Disease
  • Autoimmune Disease and Allergy
  • Cancer

Required Elements of the Proposal

The proposal should consist of the following sections:

1. Title Page and Project Summary. Give your proposal a title and put your name on it. Then, briefly descibe your proposed work (one paragraph), including why your proposal has intellectual merit. In addition, include in your Project Summary an explicit statement indicating the broader impacts of your proposed reaearch. In other words, how will your work benefit others outside of your research group.

2. A Project Description. In this section, you should introduce the reader to your topic and provide some background on what is known about the research question you seek to address. Use the sections of Gluckman et al.'s Principles of Evolutionary Medicine 2nd Ed. listed below as starting points for your research. Then, use the references cited within that section of Gluckman et al. to gain access to literature relevant to your proposal. Be sure also to indicate why yours is an evolutionary medicine topic, including an explicit connection between the phenomenon you are studying and specific evolutionary concepts and principles.

Ageing and Senescence

Ch. 5, pp. 97-107

Evolution of Infectious Disease

Ch. 10, pp. 240-244

Autoimmune Disease and Allergy

Ch. 10, pp. 245-252

Cancer

Ch. 12. pp. 287-302

You should next indicate the hypothesis you are testing in your research and the associated specific research question(s) that you will address. For example, I may want to test the hypothesis that constant exposure to freeway traffic noise leads to higher levels of schizophrenia. A specific research question I might ask is whether people who currently live closer to freeways have higher levels of schizophrenia.

Finally, indicate how you will carry out your research. What activities will you do to answer your research question(s) and test your hypothesis? Be sure to include how you will interpret the results of data that you collect. For this part of the Project Description, I anticipate that you will be guided by the methods employed by other scientists in papers that you consult for your project.

3. A References Cited section. We expect that your paper will include 6-10 books and/or primary literature sources, although some students may need more. Use either MLA or APA (my preferred format) style and guidelines for your references.
