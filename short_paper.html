Evolutionary Medicine Short Paper - A Wikipedia Entry

In this short-medium writing exercise (800 - 1000 words; 4 pages, double-spaced), each student will create a Wikipedia-style "entry" describing a human health condition of your choice from an Evolutionary Medicine perspective. The main feature of your "entry" will be the description, application, and synthesis of two or more of the core concepts of Evolutionary Medicine shown in the list below. In addition to providing the first chance for you to write formally for the course, this exercise will give you a chance to wrap your head around some core concepts and explore how they apply to a subject you find to be of interest. This short writing exercise also provides a mechanism for students to engage with Nesse and Williams' book, Why We Get Sick,, and from there the primary literature.

Required Elements

There are six core evolutionary medicine concepts that you can choose from to explore in this paper. At a minimum, choose two of these for use in your "entry". These are:

  • Life History and Trade-offs
  • Phylogeny and the Tree of Life
  • Antagonistic Pleiotropy
  • Proximate and Ultimate Evolutionary Explanations
  • Environmental Mismatch
  • Mutations and Mutation Load

Your entry should consist of the following:

1. The Health Phenomenon. What is the human health problem/phenomenon that you are describing? (If you are struggling to find a phenomenon, look through WWGS. There are tons!) As an example, I might choose "opioid addiction". In this case, I would spend some time describing for my readers what opioid addiction is, its biological and HPS dimensions (e.g., how much of a problem it is societally) and other aspects of opioid addiction that I find worth mentioning.

2. Evolutionary Medicine Concepts - Defined. In this section, you should introduce the reader to the specific evolutionary medicine concepts and principles listed above that you will be applying to your health problem/phenomenon. For each one, describe what it is, how it works/what it entails, and provide an example of its operation in a system that is NOT the one you are using for your Wikipedia entry!

3. Evolutionary Medicine Concepts - Applied and Synthesized. Following your descriptions above, you should next describe for the reader the explicit connection(s) between the phenomenon you are studying and your evolutionary medicine concepts. The best entries will not only make connections of the concepts to the phenomena, but will point out relationships and connections between the evolutionary medicine concepts themselves.

4. References Cited section. We expect that your paper will include 6-10 books and/or primary literature sources, although some students may need more. Use either MLA or APA (my preferred format) style and guidelines for your references.
