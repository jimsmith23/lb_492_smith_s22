LB492 S22 - Evolutionary Medicine
Final Project - Annotated Bibliography
Instructions and Sample

Note: This Sample Annotated Bibliography was developed by Alice Dreger for an upper division HPS course in Lyman Briggs. It is reproduced here with permission, and is intended for your use in formulating your proposal for LB492 S22.

Instructions for the Annotated Bibliography:

1. Your bibliography should include at least six primary literature sources (see me if you need an exception to this rule) arranged alphabetically, and should be annotated. You may list more than six sources in your annotated bibliography.

2.Each entry in your bibliography should be annotated. In other words, you should state in 2-5 sentences how you will use that particular source in your researched essay. (See the sample.)

3. Note that, for your bibliography, you should consistently use one of the major citation systems (APA or MLA).

 

[Sample Annotated Bibliography:]

"The Limits of Individuality: An Analysis of the Social and Medical Treatment of Conjoined Twins," by Alice D. Dreger

[Note that this annotated bibliography follows the MLA style.  You may use either the MLA or APA style, but you should chose one and stick to it!  For samples of both styles, see RHH chap. 7.]

Annas, George J.  "Siamese Twins:  Killing One to Save the Other."  Hastings Center Report 17 (April, 1987):  27-29.  (This article discusses the various ethical approaches one could employ to try to justify the intentional killing of a conjoined twin.  One of the approaches it presents is the so-called "monster approach."  I argue in my paper that this is the system employed in the current medical treatment of conjoined twins, and that this is the wrong system to use.)

Bogdan, Robert.  Freak Show:  Presenting Human Oddities for Amusement and Profit.  Chicago: University of Chicago Press, 1988.  (This book presents the history of freak shows in America, with a focus on the nineteenth and early twentieth centuries.  It demonstrates that so-called "freaks" made good livings exhibiting themselves in "freak shows."  The author argues that the present-day treatment of developmentally-different or developmentally-challenged people is different from that of the past, but not necessarily more humane or more accepting.  In my paper I agree with Bogdan on this point.)

Hubbard, Kim.  "A Gift of Grace."  People 34 (1991):  42-44.  (This article tells the story of Verena and Ruth Cady, conjoined twin sisters who died in 1991 at the age of seven.  It includes a summary of an interview with Marlene Cady, the twins' mother.  I use this article because it demonstrates that conjoined twins can lead fairly "normal" and very rewarding lives.)

Little, Miles.  Humane Medicine.  Cambridge:  Cambridge University Press, 1995.  (The author of this book is a surgeon.  Although he does not directly discuss the treatment of conjoined twins, he does discuss how doctors have gotten so caught up in medical technological fixes for every "problem" that they sometimes forget about the needs of their patients.  I use this book to explain the root causes of unethical separation surgeries.)

Pancoast, William H.  "Report on the Surgical Considerations in Regard to the Propriety of an Operation for the Separation of Eng and Chang Bunker, Commonly Known as the Siamese Twins."  Transactions of the College of Physicians 1 (1875):  149-169.  (This article contains a lot of first- and second-hand information about the lives of Chang and Eng Bunker.)
