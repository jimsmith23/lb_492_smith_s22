LB492 S17 Evolutionary Medicine
Final Paper Grading Rubric

Total - 100 Points

1. Procedural and Structural Elements (10 points)

a. Have all of the required elements of the final project been turned in on time and in the appropriate manner?

b. Is the paper the appropriate length (too long? too short?)

c. Has the author provided a paragraph describing their responses to their peer-reviewer's comments?

 

2. How well does the author lay out the Research Problem, the general approach for addressing it, and the conclusions drawn from the study? (25 points)

a. How well does the paper introduce their topic?

b. How well does the author state their burning question/thesis?

c. How well does the author describe their general approach for addressing their thesis?

d. How well does the author describe the conclusions drawn based on their research?

 

3. How well does the author organize the major parts of the paper and the ideas contained within each of them? (10 points)

a. How well are the ideas and the major themes of the paper organized?

b. Is there a logical flow of ideas between the different aspects of the paper? E.g., how well does the "story line" hang together? Are the ideas nicely connected?

 

4. How well does the author use evidence to support claims? (40 points)

a. Has the author shown clear evidence that the primary literature has been consulted and incorporated into the research paper?

b. How much reading has the author done, as evidenced by the strength of the references cited?

c. Has the author chosen and used a logical citation system consisting of in text citations and a complete references list?

 

5. Clear, Concise, and Correct Language Usage (10 points)

a. How well has the author used language, including (but not limited to) sentence structure, grammar and spelling?

 

6. Interesting Paper? (5 points)

a. Has the author made an effort to make their paper interesting to read, both thematically and in their use of prose?
