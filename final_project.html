LB492 S22
Final Project - Researched Essay
Outline, Expectations, Due Dates and Point Allocation

A. Components of the Final Project (100 Points Total)

During the week of May 2, 2022, each student must turn in each of the following (via D2L):

1. Final Paper (50 points)

2. Proposal (20 points)

3. Annotated Bibliography (10 Points)

3. The Paper Draft and the Peer Review Exercise (20 Points)

B. Descriptions of the Components

1. The Final Paper (50 points); Due during the week of May 2, 2022

a. Overview

The final product of this series of exercises will be a 12- to 15-page masterpiece (double-spaced, one inch margins; aim for 3,000 - 3,500 words, excluding references). This paper should address a topic derived or connected to the issues and ideas discussed in our Evolutionary Medicine Senior Seminar. Students should examine their topic from multiple perspectives (e.g., the natural sciences, the social sciences, and/or the humanities)

2. The Proposal (20 Points); due Monday March 29, 2022

Your proposal should be 2.5-3.0 pages long (double-spaced, one inch margins; aim for 650 - 750 words) and should lay out what you plan to do in your researched essay.

a. In addition to providing a summary and overview of your final researched essay, your proposal should do the following:
  • State what your topic is, as specifically as possible.
  • List what question(s) you will attempt to answer in the paper.
  • Name the kinds of sources you will use and discuss how you will use them.
  • Explain why this is a Capstone Senior Seminar paper.
  • Explain why this is an original critical analysis.

b. You will need to have read a number of your intended sources in order to write your proposal. You will certainly find that you will need to add extra sources as you complete your researched essay project.

c. Give the researched essay project a tentative title, and put that title at the top of your proposal.

d. You may write about your paper in the present tense or in the future tense for purposes of the proposal. I recommend the present tense.

e. The following link is to a sample proposal (written by former LBC HPS Prof. Alice Dreger). You do not need to lay out your proposal exactly as it is here. You should organize your proposal in a way that makes sense for your project.

f. Proposals are due by Tuesday March 29, 2022. Submission should be via D2L, and early submission is encouraged. I will examine submitted proposals and hold individual conferences with students as necessary (and as desired). 

3. Annotated Bibliography (10 Points)

a. Overview

The annotated bibliography is a fleshed-out set of references that you will incorporate into your paper.

b. Annotated Bibliography Workshop; Tuesday March 22, 2022

The workshop will help students understand how the Annotated Bibliography will be layered onto and incorporated into the Final Paper

c. Instructions for preparation of the Annotated Bibliography:

  • Your bibliography should include at least six primary literature sources (see me if you need an exception to this rule) arranged alphabetically, and should be annotated. You may list more than six sources in your annotated bibliography.
  • Each entry in your bibliography should be annotated. In other words, you should state in 2-5 sentences how you will use that particular source in your researched essay. (See the sample.)
  • Note that, for your bibliography, you should consistently use one of the major citation systems (APA or MLA).
  • Your Annotated Bibliography is due with the final paper the week of May 2, 2022
  • The following link is to a sample annotated bibliography (written by Alice Dreger). Most elements of this sample bibliography apply to your own.

4. Paper Draft and Peer Review (20 Points); Tuesday April 19, 2022

Share a copy of your draft with your peer-editor and Dr. Smith for this session!!!

a. Overview

On Tuesday April 19 we will have a peer editing session on drafts of your final papers. Students will be asked to assess how well their classmates have integrated the various elements of their project (i.e. proposal and annotated bibliography) into their final papers. The peer review sheets and peer reviewed drafts will be turned in with the final paper and will be graded. Your Draft will be worth 10 points (showing up with an editable draft) and your performance as a peer reviewer will be worth 10 points.
