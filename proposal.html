Sample Proposal

"The 'Sacrifice' of Conjoined Twins in Society and Medicine"

by Alice D. Dreger

 

[FIRST, I INTRODUCE MY TOPIC:]  Today many medical professionals, especially U.S. surgeons, appear willing to take great risks in order to separate conjoined ("Siamese") twins.  Some surgical separations are fairly simple and can be performed with little risk to the twins.  But an increasing number of doctors are willing to attempt separations even if the separation is highly likely to leave twins brain-damaged, immobile, badly scarred, without reproductive capabilities they once had, with a sex-change, or even dead.  In fact, more and more surgeons are willing to perform "sacrifice" surgeries in which the surgeons intentionally asphyxiate one of two conscious conjoined twins.  In no other realm of medicine can doctors legally or ethically induce brain-death in a brain-live person without that person's explicit consent.

 

[THEN I STATE MY "BURNING QUESTION" FOR THIS PAPER:]  How did this unparalleled type of medical treatment arise, and why is it ethically wrong?

 

[NOW I MORE CLEARLY SPECIFY MY TOPIC:]  In this paper I review the history of the social and medical treatment of conjoined twins, and I demonstrate that today's medical treatment of conjoined twins reflects the long-standing and extremely negative social attitudes towards conjoined twins.  Specifically the social attitude has been that conjoined twinning is monstrous, and the medical treatment has acted as if that is so; conjoined twins have been subjected to medical procedures not normally applied to humans, and in effect they have been treated as monstrous or not fully human.  In this paper I argue that this is ethically wrong.

 

[I EXPLAIN WHY THIS IS AN HPS TOPIC:] This is an HPS paper because I show how the social realms (social attitudes towards the abnormal) have influenced medical professionals to treat a naturally occurring human variation (conjoined twinning) in a particularly negative way.  I document, analyze, and critique this system.  I am addressing the themes of "questions of authority" (who counts as an authority, medical professionals or conjoined twins themselves), "cultural influences on medicine and medicine's influence on culture" and "theory-ladenness of observation" (how observations of conjoined twins are shaped by beliefs about what counts as normal).

 

[I EXPLAIN WHY THIS IS A LEVEL-4 PAPER:]  Other people have written about the biology of conjoined twinning and about the personal lives of conjoined twins.  They have also discussed some of the ethical problems associated with the cost of conjoined twin separations.  But I'm going beyond all approaches these to a new level:  I am arguing in this paper that "sacrifice" separations of conjoined twins are fundamentally based on profoundly negative social attitudes.  I argue that "sacrifice" surgeries are wrong.  I show that twins who remain conjoined can live fulfilling lives, and moreover that they are fully human and therefore deserving of being treated with the same ethical and legal standards as everyone else.In this paper to make my argument I am using my own ideas about what it means to be human and what medicine should and shouldn't aim to do.

 

[DISCUSSION OF MY SOURCES:]  To set up my main argument (that conjoined twins should be treated according to the same ethical principles as everyone else and that sacrifice surgeries are wrong) I have to present my reader with factual historical information which shows two things: first, that the social and medical treatment of conjoined twins has often been harmful and de-humanizing; second, that conjoined twins themselves have managed to live rewarding, meaningful lives.  To do this, I use information I have gathered from biographies of conjoined twins.  I also use historical articles and books which discuss the history of cultural attitudes towards "freaks."  I show the reader of my paper that conjoined twins have often been treated as "freaks" but that this is a bigoted and unjustified attitude. 

 

[MORE ABOUT MY SOURCES AND HOW I USE THEM:]  Since this paper is primarily about the medical treatment of conjoined twins, I have to take a close look at the medical treatment of conjoined twins.  Therefore in this paper I also use information gleaned from history of medicine texts and from reports published in medical journals by the surgeons themselves.  Using these texts I show how the medical approach consistently treats conjoined twinning as an impossible way to live, something disproved by the biographies of conjoined twins themselves.  I also use the medical record to show that "sacrifice" surgeries involve the intentional killing of a conscious person.

 

[MORE ABOUT MY SOURCES AND HOW I USE THEM:]  In order to show that my ethical analysis is unique and original, I have to review what other ethical analyses of separations surgeries have said.In my review of ethics papers, I show that other authors have not recognized that (1) conjoined twins often describe their lives as normal and meaningful, and (2) conjoined twins must be accorded the same ethical and legal protections as everyone else.

 

REMEMBER: You do not need to lay out your prospectus exactly as I have here. You should organize your prospectus in a way that makes sense for your project.
